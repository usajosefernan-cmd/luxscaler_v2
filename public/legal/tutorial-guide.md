## ðŸŽ›ï¸ Advanced Settings Explained

### Understanding the Sliders

#### 1. **Creativity (0-10)**

**What it does:**
- **0:** Perfect 1:1 restoration (no changes)
- **5:** Balanced (some enhancement, preserves original)
- **10:** Full reimagining (very artistic, may change appearance)

**When to use:**
- **Low (0-3):** Portraits, text, documents (preserve identity)
- **Medium (5-6):** Most photos (nice balance)
- **High (8-10):** CGI, artistic, landscape (creative freedom)

**Example:**
```
Original: Faded old portrait
Creativity 2: Cleaned up but still looks like original person
Creativity 8: More dramatic, enhanced colors, artistic style
```

#### 2. **Fractality (0-10)**

**What it does:**
- **0:** Very smooth, soft, painterly
- **5:** Normal detail level
- **10:** Hyper-sharp, every detail visible (sometimes too much)

**When to use:**
- **Low (2-4):** Portraits (smooth skin), old photos (soften damage)
- **Medium (5-6):** General purpose, balanced
- **High (8-10):** Landscapes, products, architecture (show detail)

**Example:**
```
Original: Soft skin with some texture
Fractality 3: Smooth, glowing skin (flattering)
Fractality 8: Every pore visible (professional but intense)
```

#### 3. **Light Boost (-5 to +5)**

**What it does:**
- **-5:** Darken shadows, add drama
- **0:** Keep original lighting
- **+5:** Brighten, reveal detail in dark areas

**When to use:**
- **Negative:** Dark, moody photos (strengthen shadows)
- **Zero:** Well-lit photos
- **Positive:** Dark/shadowed photos (portraits indoors, nighttime)

**Example:**
```
Original: Selfie taken in poor indoor lighting (dark face)
Light Boost +3: Face becomes bright, details visible
```

#### 4. **Color Boost (-5 to +5)**

**What it does:**
- **-5:** Desaturate (black & white, faded look)
- **0:** Keep original colors
- **+5:** Hyper-vibrant, saturated colors

**When to use:**
- **Negative:** Overexposed/blown-out colors (mute them)
- **Zero:** Well-balanced photos
- **Positive:** Faded photos, landscapes (make colors pop)

**Example:**
```
Original: Landscape with dull, faded sky
Color Boost +4: Sky becomes rich blue, grass vivid green
```

#### 5. **Sharpness Boost (-5 to +5)**

**What it does:**
- **-5:** Soften (anti-aliasing effect)
- **0:** Natural sharpness
- **+5:** Maximum sharpness (like "Max Sharpness" preset)

**When to use:**
- **Negative:** Oversharpened images, noisy photos
- **Zero:** Normal photos
- **Positive:** Soft/blurry photos, products, landscapes

**Example:**
```
Original: Slightly blurry vacation photo
Sharpness +4: Every detail becomes crisp
```

## ðŸ› ï¸ Common Issues & Solutions

### Issue 1: "My face looks different in the 4K output"

**Why it happens:**
- Creativity was set too high (>5)
- AI emphasized facial features

**Solution:**
- Use **Creativity 2-4** for portraits
- Choose preview that matches your face best
- Use "Balanced Polish" preset (conservative style)

---

### Issue 2: "The image is too blurry/soft"

**Why it happens:**
- Fractality was set too low (<4)
- Original image was very low resolution

**Solution:**
- Increase **Fractality to 7-8**
- Use "Max Sharpness" preview
- Try "Forensic Recovery" if original is damaged

---

### Issue 3: "Colors look oversaturated/fake"

**Why it happens:**
- Color Boost was set too high (>+4)
- Image was already color-graded

**Solution:**
- Reduce **Color Boost to +2 or 0**
- Use "Balanced Polish" (conservative color)
- Use "Trend" if you want artistic colors

---

### Issue 4: "Lighting looks weird/artificial"

**Why it happens:**
- Light Boost was set too extreme (>+4 or <-4)
- Original lighting was inconsistent

**Solution:**
- Keep **Light Boost between -2 and +3**
- Use "Studio Lighting" preview (professionally balanced)
- Choose a preview that matches your intended style

---

### Issue 5: "Text/details look AI-generated"

**Why it happens:**
- Creativity was too high (AI "imagined" details)
- Image type is complex (mixed text + photo)

**Solution:**
- Set **Creativity to 0-1** for documents
- Use "Forensic Recovery" (safe, minimal changes)
- Avoid "Creative Reality" or "Max Sharpness"
